The A. Lange & Söhne 1815 Rattrapante Perpetual Calendar (Reference 421.056FE) is a masterpiece that combines three sophisticated complications: a rattrapante chronograph, a perpetual calendar, and a moon-phase display. Housed in an 18-carat white gold case, this limited edition timepiece exemplifies the brand's commitment to precision and elegance.

Design and Features:

  • Case: 41.9 mm diameter, 14.7 mm thickness, crafted from 18-carat white gold. It features sapphire crystal front and case back, providing a clear view of the intricate movement.
  • Dial: Solid 18-carat pink gold with a matte finish. It displays hours, minutes, and subsidiary seconds with stop seconds. The dial also features a rattrapante chronograph with minute counter, perpetual calendar with date, day of the week, month, moon-phase, and leap year indicators, along with a power-reserve indicator.
  • Movement: Manual-winding caliber L101.1, consisting of 631 parts. It offers a power reserve of 42 hours and operates at a frequency of 21,600 semi-oscillations per hour (3.0 Hz).
  • Strap: Hand-stitched dark brown alligator leather strap with an 18-carat white gold deployant buckle, ensuring both comfort and sophistication.
